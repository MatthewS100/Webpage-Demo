Index Funds

Some information about Index Funds and why you should invest in them.

Some basic information about index funds

An index fund is a portfolio of stocks or bonds designed to mimic the composition and performance of a financial market index. Index funds have lower expenses and fees than actively managed funds. Index funds follow a passive investment strategy.

Why Index Funds?

  • Lower Costs than actively managed funds. For example, an index fund fee is usually between 0.03% to 0.5%, while an actively managed funds are usually between 1.0% to 2.5%.
  • Allows you to diversify in multiple companies that are composed of said index, for example the S&P 500.
  • One of famed investor Warren Buffet recommended investments.
  • One of the easiest ways to become a potential millionaire. For example, if you invest a 1000 dollars into a S&P 500 index fund, and you get a annual 7 percent return per year, along with investing 400 dollars per month you will have 973,223 dollars in 40 years!
